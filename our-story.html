Building After the Storm

When I left Ukraine in 2022, I carried only a bag of clothes and thirty years of memories. In Kyiv, I was an architect. In America, I began again as a laborer. But the passion for structural integrity and the beauty of a well-laid stone never left my hands.

I remember the first bathroom I remodeled here. The client wanted something modern, but I saw they needed something timeless. I spent three extra days ensuring the grout lines were microscopic, a technique my grandfather taught me in the outskirts of Lviv.
